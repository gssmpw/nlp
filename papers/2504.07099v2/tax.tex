\section{Taxonomy}
This section offers a detailed and nuanced exploration of the research landscape surrounding frequency-domain machine learning.  To provide a clear and comprehensive overview, we present a two-tiered taxonomy in Figure~\ref{fig_taxonomy1} and Figure~\ref{fig_taxonomy1}. Figure~\ref{fig_taxonomy1} offers a detailed classification of existing studies, meticulously categorizing them based on key characteristics and methodologies. % This detailed taxonomy allows for a fine-grained understanding of the specific approaches employed within the field. This broader perspective complements the detailed taxonomy in Figure~\ref{fig_taxonomy2}, allowing readers to grasp the overall structure and relationships between different research areas.

To enhance understanding and facilitate comparison, Table ~\ref{tab:training_ablation} presents a comparative analysis of various representation methods. It categorizes methods based on their underlying frequency transform, specific methodological approach, and the publication venue scope. This analysis allows direct comparison of approaches, highlighting strengths and weaknesses in different contexts.

% Finally, to illustrate the temporal evolution of research within specific application tasks, we present Figure~\ref{figure_time}. This figure provides a clear visual representation of how different frequency transforms have been applied to various tasks over time.  This chronological perspective highlights the progression of research, identifying key trends and advancements within the field.  By combining these visual and tabular representations, we aim to provide the reader with a thorough and accessible understanding of the current state of research in frequency-domain machine learning.
