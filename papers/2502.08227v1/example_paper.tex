\section*{Impact Statement}

improved understanding of how different mislabeled data affect deep learning models has significant implications for real-world applications where label noise is prevalent, such as large-scale data annotation, crowd-sourcing, and autonomous data collection systems. By reducing the overlook of particularly harmful mislabeled examples, our method can lead to more reliable and accurate models in critical domains like healthcare, finance, and autonomous driving.
However, it is important to acknowledge potential limitations and ethical considerations. Our approach relies on assumptions about the learning dynamics of deep neural networks, which may not hold uniformly across all architectures or data distributions. There is a risk that some valuable but challenging data could be inadvertently discarded, potentially introducing biases or reducing model fairness.
