\begin{abstract}

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have shown impressive performance in
vision and text tasks.  However, hallucination remains a major challenge,
especially in fields like healthcare where details are critical.
In this work, we show how MLLMs may be enhanced to support Visual RAG
(V-RAG), a retrieval-augmented generation framework that incorporates
both text and visual data from retrieved images.  On the MIMIC-CXR chest X-ray
report generation and Multicare medical image caption generation datasets,
we show that Visual RAG improves the accuracy of entity probing,
which asks whether a medical entities is grounded by an image.
We show that the improvements extend both to frequent and rare entities, the latter of which may have less positive training data.
Downstream, we apply V-RAG with entity probing to correct hallucinations and
generate more clinically accurate X-ray reports, obtaining a higher
RadGraph-F1 score.

% Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have shown impressive performance in vision-and-text tasks, particularly in healthcare, where Medical MLLMs (Med-MLLMs) assist in clinical decision-making. However, hallucination remains a major challenge, especially in critical fields like healthcare. In this work, we introduce Visual-RAG (V-RAG), a retrieval-augmented generation framework that incorporates both text and visual data from retrieved images to mitigate hallucination in Med-MLLMs. By utilizing disease entity probing—a method of evaluating disease-related entities in medical reports—we show that V-RAG improves model accuracy compared to existing text-based RAG methods. Additionally, we introduce fine-tuning strategies that improve the model's multimodal comprehension and allow Med-MLLMs trained on single-image datasets to leverage multi-image inputs. Experiments on two medical datasets confirm the effectiveness of V-RAG in reducing hallucination and improving inference across diverse scenario.
\end{abstract}
