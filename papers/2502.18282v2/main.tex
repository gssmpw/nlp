\section{Conclusion}
We introduced a pipeline to investigate political leaning in the pretraining corpora, which allows us to compare the LLMs' political leaning not only with surveyed human opinions but also the political leanings embedded in their pretraining corpora. By examining LLMs' stances on political issues derived from U.S. Supreme Court cases, our results reveal a significant alignment between the models and their training corpora, yet no similarly strong alignment with human opinions as survyed is found. We call on the AI community to explore methods for rigorous evaluation of LLM alignment, and advocate for more transparent and collaborative strategies in curating training data for LLM. 
% We have provided an analysis of different human-LLM alignment evaluation metrics \ye{is this the main point of the paper?}. We introduced the significance test for both instance-level and system-level metrics to calculate the differences in LLMs’ alignment with different reference groups \ye{we don't introduce this test. we simply use it. and again, i wouldn't make such a big deal out of it}. We also introduced pipeline to investigate political leaning in the pretraining corpora, which allows us to compare the LLMs' political leaning not only with different the court and various human demographic groups, but also their training corpora \ye{this part for instance is much more improtant than the use of a statistical test}. We empirically of LLMs’ preference on political issues from the US supreme court cases. Our analysis of statistical significance shows that some LLMs are significantly aligned to the training corpora, but there no insufficient evidence for a significant alignment between human and LLMs.
