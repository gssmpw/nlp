\section{Instances from the Multilingual and Cross-lingual SoC Benchmarks}
\label{sec:appendix:stelfig}

In our multilingual SoC benchmark, anchor ($a$) has the same style and different content from a positive example ({\tt pos}), and the same content but different style from a negative example ({\tt neg}). The anchor and the {\tt pos} and {\tt neg} sentences are in the same language. The tested model needs to successfully pair $a$ with {\tt pos} (rather than $a$ and {\tt neg}). Cross-lingual SoC has the same setup as multilingual SoC, except that the {\tt pos} and {\tt neg} examples are in a different language than the anchor. Figure \ref{fig:both_figures} contains instances of each benchmark.
