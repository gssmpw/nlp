\begin{abstract}
%Text-to-audio generation models (TAG) such as AudioGen~\cite{kreuk2022audiogen} have achieved significant advances in generating audio conditioned on text descriptions. However, these models often lack transparency, making it difficult for users to understand how specific text tokens influence the generated audio. To address this issue, we introduce \mname{}, an Explainable AI (XAI) framework designed to enhance the interpretability of AudioGen-based models. \mname{} leverages both factual and counterfactual reasoning to quantify the importance of each text token to provide faithful explanations of the generated audio. By decomposing the complex task of explaining sequential audio generation into manageable subproblems, \mname{} delivers insights at both the audio token level and the entire audio. Through extensive experiments, we demonstrate the effectiveness of our method and benchmark it against recent explanation techniques using proposed evaluation metrics tailored for audio generation tasks. Our results highlight \mname{}'s ability to significantly enhance the faithful explainability of TAG models. Our code is available at \url{https://anonymous.4open.science/r/audiogenX/}.
