We use $\hat{A}^\star_i$ to denote $\max_{j\in[m]}\hat{A}_{i,j}$. 
\begin{align*}
    |\hat{L}(\lambda, \pi^t) - L(\lambda^*, \pi^*)|&=
    \bigg|\sum_{i=1}^n\langle \hat{A_i}, \pi^t\rangle-\sum_{i=1}^n\langle A_i, \pi^*\rangle+\sum_{i=1}^n\lambda_i (\langle \hat{A}_i, \pi^t \rangle-c\hat{A}^\star_i)-\sum_{i=1}^n\lambda^*_i (\langle A_i, \pi^* \rangle-c\Amax_i)\bigg|
\end{align*}
We want to show that $|L(\lambda, \pi^t) - L(\lambda^*, \pi^*)|\leq \tilde{O}(\sqrt{T})$ and an appropriate function of both social welfare and fairness regrets are upper-bounded by $|L(\lambda, \pi^t) - L(\lambda^*, \pi^*)|$.
